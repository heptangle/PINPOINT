PINPOINT pursues three interconnected research themes — each a different dimension of how domestic production networks shape the politics of economic globalisation.

Research themes
01

Production Networks & Government Behaviour

How does the structure of domestic production networks shape governments' policy responses to economic globalisation? This theme investigates the conditions under which governments adopt protective or liberalising trade policies as a function of the network ties among domestic firms.

02

Citizen Preferences & Network Exposure

To what extent do production-network ties mediate the formation of individual preferences over trade and globalisation? This theme develops and tests new theoretical models of how indirect exposure to global markets — through supply-chain linkages — shapes mass preferences.

03

Institutions & the Architecture of Networks

How do political institutions shape the architecture of domestic production networks, and how do these networks in turn reshape institutional outcomes? This theme examines the two-way relationship between institutions and production network structures.

Empirical approach

Data & Methods

PINPOINT combines original cross-country datasets on firm-level production ties with survey experiments, text analysis, and quasi-experimental designs to provide robust empirical tests of the theoretical framework.
